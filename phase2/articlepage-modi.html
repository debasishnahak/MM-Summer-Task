PM Modi Speaks At Madison Square Garden: Highlights

NEW YORK: Prime Minister Narendra Modi, in a saffron jacket, got a full-house reception on Sunday at New York's Madison Square Garden, a venue that rock stars dream of packing with a sell-out audience. He thanked the crowd of about 18,000 Indian Americans who constantly chanted his name, for the rousing welcome.
Here are the highlights of his speech:

  1. Prime Minister Narendra Modi arrives at Madison Square Garden
  2. Kavita Krishnamurthy sings the national anthem
  3. Mr Modi begins his address with 'Bharat Mata ki Jai'
  4. My greetings to you on the occasion of Navratri. I am fortunate to have this opportunity to meet you on such an auspicious occasions
  5. India used to be known as a country of snakes and snake charmers
  6. It is only because of you (Indian-American community) that we have made such a huge progress
  7. If not for you all,there wouldn't have been an IT revolution
  8. You (Indian-American community) have earned a lot of respect in USA through skills and values
  9. Through your actions and values you have earned immense respect
  10. I visited Taiwan once and an interpreter said your country has been a land of snakes and snake charmers
  11. I said 'No, we have been vastly under-valued. Our ancestors used to play with snakes, we play with mouse.'
  12. Many of you didn't get a chance to vote, but when the results came, not one of you must have been sleeping
  13. Many of you were involved in the election process
  14. You may not have voted in 2014 but when results were coming in, you celebrated
  15. Today, I am here to thank you all
  16. You have a huge contribution in building India's image.
  17. Winning an election is not just about post or chair, it's a responsibility
  18. I haven't taken a 15-minute break since I took over.
  19. For the first time in 30 years, a party won a clear majority in India
  20. Even the opinion makers could not make an opinion
  21. You all have several expectations from India. Even the people of India are expecting a lot from our government.
  22. We will be successful in fulfilling the aspirations of people
  23. The world is convinced that 21st century belongs to Asia
  24. We are a youthful nation with a very old culture. 65 per cent of Indians are below the age of 35.
  25. There is no reason to be disappointed. India will progress very fast and the skills of our youth will take the country ahead.
  26. When 125 crore people give their blessings, it automatically becomes God's blessing.
  27. We have three powers that nobody else has, and it is our duty to identify these powers, present it to the World and mobilise it: Democracy - This is our biggest strength. Even the poorest man went out in the heat to hear speeches Demographic dividend - 65% of people less than the age of 35 years Demand - The World is looking at India
  28. Democracy is India's biggest strength
  29. Mahatma Gandhi made the freedom struggle a mass movement. Every Indian felt that yes, I am also a part of the freedom struggle. We want to make development a mass movement.
  30. The world needs teachers, we will supply teachers
  31. Mahatma Gandhi was an expatriate who returned to India. It will be 100 years of that event next year. Pravasi Bahartiya Divas will be held in Ahmedabad this time
  32. Those who stay in India for a long time had to report to police stations. They will not have to do so.
